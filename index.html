An interactive installation based on audience's motion

SUBSTANCES is an interactive installation made for UCSD ICAM SENIOR EXHIBITION. The installation is consist of four parts, Fire, Earth, Water, and Air, which are the four basic elements in the Ancient alchemy. In the theory of alchemy, the four basic elements make up everything in this world. This work hopes to give people thoughts about the composition of the world. When the viewer stands in front of this work, they can clearly feel the characteristics of the different elements in each mode and interact with the screen in real time. When people realize that the ability they get in the work is actually the ability that nature already has, will they realize that nature, like the audience manipulating things in the work, easily damaged the civilization and order that human beings are proud of in a short period of time through the invisible virus? The work indirectly creates some stimulation for people to think about the relationship between man and nature.


Year:2022

Keyword: interactive art, real-time render, particle art


background information

According to Alchemy, the world is made up of four elements, Air, Earth, Water and Fire. Air represents the ability to guide and pull everything, Earth represents the vitality that can nourish life, Water represents the fluidity and uncertainty of things, and Fire represents the powerful destructive and reshaping power. The whole installation will be divided into four modes to present the different characteristics of the four elements. In each mode, viewers can interact with the canvas through their motions.
